## Why Markets Moved

### 1. Tech Malaise Week Concludes — Rotation Accelerates

The dominant narrative all week was rotation out of mega-cap tech and into cyclicals, industrials, and small caps. Friday's session was no exception:

- **Nasdaq** posted its 4th consecutive daily decline, capping a brutal week for tech. The Nasdaq Composite finished the week with losses in every session.
- **Breadth improved dramatically.** 63% of S&P 500 stocks now trade above their 50-day moving average, up from just 50% at the start of June. Advancing issues outnumbered decliners on multiple days this week even as the cap-weighted index fell — a classic internal rotation signal.
- **Kevin Gordon, Schwab Center for Financial Research:** "The rolling 52-week correlation between the cap-weighted and equal-weighted S&P 500 indexes is plunging and at its lowest since 2003."
- **Mag 7 carnage:** The Magnificent Seven collectively shed $2.99 trillion in market cap in June — on track for the largest monthly decline on record. Apple -6% Thursday (worst day since April 2025), Microsoft -3.5%, Nvidia on pace for an 8% weekly decline (worst in over a year).

### 2. OpenAI IPO Delay — The $1 Trillion Valuation Question

The New York Times reported that OpenAI is leaning toward postponing its IPO from late 2026 to 2027, struggling to secure sufficient demand at its targeted $1 trillion valuation. CEO Sam Altman reportedly considered any reduction from the $1T target "a nonstarter."

**Fallout:**
- **SoftBank Group** (largest foreign backer): -9.2% Friday, -12.53% at Thursday's worst
- **Oracle, CoreWeave**: fell on the report (both AI infrastructure plays)
- **Broad sentiment hit:** The delay crystallized investor fear that AI valuations have overshot realistic demand, particularly as hyperscaler costs balloon. David Makaryan, Alpha Pacific Group (via BBC): "The long-term investment case for AI remains compelling, but investors are becoming far more selective about which companies can justify the valuations the market has assigned to them."

### 3. Oil Plunge — Peace Trade Outruns Physical Reality

WTI crude fell 3.8% to $69.20 — the lowest since before the Iran conflict escalated in late February. Brent at $72.47 (-3.7%). Drivers:

- **Supply returning:** Saudi tankers resumed Persian Gulf exports. US Treasury issued a 60-day general license authorizing Iranian-origin crude transactions (valid until Aug 21).
- **OPEC+ still pumping:** +188K b/d in June, another +188K b/d planned for July.
- **Demand weakening:** EIA slashed 2026 global demand growth forecast by ~1.1M b/d.
- **But the Strait isn't normal:** A cargo vessel was struck off Oman on June 25. Saudi Aramco CEO warned full normalization may take until 2027. UBS called the selloff an "overreaction" — sees Brent ending 2026 at $85.

### 4. Consumer Sentiment Rebounds from Record Lows

Michigan Consumer Sentiment final June reading: **49.5**, up from May's 44.8 (+10.5% MoM). Still the second-lowest reading on record, but the direction surprised to the upside. Gasoline price moderation cited as the key driver. Current conditions: 47.7 (May: 45.8), Expectations: 50.6.
