## Senior PM Recap
**Post Market:** risk-on relief rally, but the tape still has a quality problem.
- **Tape:** S&P 500 +1.18%, Nasdaq +2.07%, Dow +0.59% to a record close above 52,000; VIX -4.78%. This was a high-beta/mega-cap rebound after a five-session drawdown, not a clean all-clear signal.
- **Driver:** US-Iran de-escalation removed the immediate Hormuz tail risk, forcing a safe-haven unwind: gold -1.62%, dollar -0.26%, vol lower. That gave AI/momentum permission to bounce after last week's Nasdaq -4.5% flush.
- **Street/context:** Sell-side remains constructive but conditional: RBC lifted its 12-month S&P target to 8,150; JPMorgan is at 7,800 on stronger EPS/AI capex but warns the path is non-linear; Goldman says the next catalyst is Q2 earnings because the last year’s S&P return was earnings-driven.
- **Risk:** Breadth was weak under the headline: NYSE decliners outnumbered advancers 1.05-to-1 and only 209 S&P names advanced. Reddit/Substack color is already leaning into “buy the war dip / rotation is back,” which is useful sentiment context but not confirmation.
- **Next watch:** Thursday payrolls decide whether this relief rally has legs. Soft enough keeps the AI/long-duration bounce alive; hot enough re-prices Warsh-Fed hike risk and exposes the narrow rally.
