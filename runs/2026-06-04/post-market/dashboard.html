Rates & Macro

Labor market holding firm. ADP reported +122K private payrolls in May (vs. 110K consensus) — the strongest print since January 2025. Gains were broad-based across 8 of 10 sectors. Education/health led (+57K), trade/transport (+36K). Wage growth: job-stayers 4.4% YoY, job-switchers 6.5%.

Friday's BLS report: consensus +80-85K nonfarm payrolls, unemployment steady at 4.3%. A beat would support the rotation thesis (economy strong enough for broadening). A miss would reignite recession fears and send capital back to tech safety.

Productivity revised down. Q1 nonfarm productivity was revised to +0.3% from +0.8% (consensus 0.8%), down from +1.6% in Q4. This is a blow to the AI-productivity narrative — if AI isn't showing up in macro productivity data yet, the capex spending may face more scrutiny.

Fed Beige Book: widespread fuel-driven inflation complaints. Higher transportation costs passing through. Fed officials "increasingly concerned" about Iran war's price impact — may need to raise rates again despite market pricing a hold. The June 16-17 FOMC is priced at near-certainty of holding 3.50-3.75%.

10Y Treasury yield: 4.48%, down 2bps. Oil's retreat provided a bid for duration. But the Beige Book's inflation warnings and Friday's jobs report keep the range tight.

Credit Markets: The Blackstone Canary

Blackstone capped withdrawals at 5% on its $79 billion flagship BCRED (private credit fund) after Q2 redemption requests hit 10% of shares — up from 7.9% in Q1. The fund shrank from $82B to $79B.

This matters because: (1) BCRED is the largest vehicle in the $2T+ private credit market; (2) it's the second consecutive quarter of elevated redemptions; (3) Partners Group and Cliffwater ($31.3B) reported similar issues this week; (4) Evercore flagged "slowing gross sales" as a "larger and more prolonged issue" for the industry.

But context is key: The 10% redemption rate was "better than feared" (Evercore). BX shares rose 8% on the news. Class I shares have delivered 9.3% annualized returns since inception — a 50% premium to leveraged loans. Blackstone argues the structure is working as designed: limiting redemptions prevents forced asset sales.

The private credit redemption wave is a slow-burn risk, not an acute crisis. Monitor for: (a) whether Q3 redemption requests accelerate; (b) whether any fund breaks the 5% cap; (c) spread widening in leveraged loans as forced sellers emerge.
