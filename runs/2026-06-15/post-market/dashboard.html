Why Markets Moved

Dominant catalyst: US-Iran peace agreement. President Trump announced late Sunday via Truth Social that a ceasefire deal with Iran was "now complete." The memorandum of understanding, mediated by Pakistan, paves the way for the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz — the chokepoint handling ~20% of global seaborne oil trade that has been restricted since the conflict began in late February (Axios, CNBC).

Trump's Truth Social post: "I hereby fully authorize the toll free opening of the Strait of Hormuz, and, simultaneously herewith, authorize the immediate removal of the United States Naval blockade. Ships of the World, start your engines! Let the oil flow!" (CNBC, TheStreet)

Key deal terms (Reuters, TheStreet, CNBC):

  • Formal signing ceremony Friday June 19 in Switzerland
  • 60-day ceasefire extension; talks to finalize within that window
  • Iran to receive half of ~$24B in frozen funds before final negotiations
  • U.S. official: funds released only after Iran demonstrates compliance
  • Israel not bound by the agreement regarding Hezbollah or Lebanon

Oil price collapse as transmission mechanism: WTI fell below $80 intraday for the first time since the conflict began (CNBC). The oil price spike had driven 10Y yields up ~50bp since late February (Investopedia). Its unwind reversed the entire geopolitical risk premium trade.

Brian Mulberry (Zacks Investment Management): "It seems real this time as both rates and oil have broken through key levels." Falling crude to $80 during FOMC week signals "we don't need to raise rates [and] that the price pressure should alleviate relatively quickly." (CNBC)

Secondary driver: SpaceX (SPCX) momentum. After Friday's record $75B IPO (largest in history), shares rose another ~19.6% Monday to approximately $180, with market cap above $2.1T. The halo effect lifted the entire tech complex, particularly semiconductors and AI names (Yahoo Finance, CNBC).

Tertiary driver: Fed preview. Markets enter the June FOMC meeting (Tue-Wed) with new Chair Kevin Warsh's first press conference. CME FedWatch prices 98% probability of no change (Yahoo Finance). The oil-price collapse weakens the rate-hike narrative.

Market holiday: NYSE/Nasdaq closed Friday June 19 for Juneteenth — compressed 4-day trading week.

The Gold Conundrum

Gold's +2.81% move alongside a massive equity rally and cratering vol is notable. In a pure risk-on day, gold should fall on reduced safe-haven demand. Instead, gold rallied on:

1. USD weakness (DXY -0.13%)

2. Falling real yields (10Y nominal down, inflation expectations likely steady-to-up on peace dividend)

3. Potential: positioning/short-covering after the geopolitical unwind

This isn't a divergence that screams "something's wrong" — it's consistent with the "rates down, dollar down" channel. But it's worth flagging because gold's reaction function appears to be shifting from safe-haven to real-rate proxy.
