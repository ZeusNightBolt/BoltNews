## Why Markets Moved

**1. Tech/Semiconductor Relief Rally Extended (Primary Driver)**
The Nasdaq led for a second straight session after snapping a five-day losing streak Monday. The VanEck Semiconductor ETF (SMH) gained ~3% intraday, bringing its Q2 surge to ~70%—a record quarterly gain. XLK closed +2.76%. AI investment enthusiasm continues to broaden: 16 of the Russell 2000's 50 best YTD performers are chip-related small caps.
*Source: CNBC, TheStreet, market_snapshot.json (XLK). Timestamp: June 30, 2026 session.*

**2. Iran-U.S. Ceasefire and Doha Talks (Risk-On Catalyst)**
President Trump announced renewed U.S.-Iran talks in Doha, Qatar on Tuesday. Both sides agreed to halt hostilities after weekend skirmishes. Priority: reopening the Strait of Hormuz to commercial vessels. Iran's foreign ministry denied direct meetings but confirmed messages exchanged via Qatari mediators. Oil fell on easing supply-disruption fears (WTI -1.13%).
*Source: Axios (June 28), Fox News (June 30), WSJ. Ceasefire announced Sunday; talks began Tuesday.*

**3. Q2/1H Window Dressing and Quarter-End Flows**
The final trading day of Q2 saw positioning flows amplify the tech bounce. With the S&P 500 +14% and Nasdaq +20% for the quarter, institutional rebalancing and performance-chasing contributed to afternoon buying.
*Source: WSJ, CNBC. Tim Hortons/Orion CIO Tim Holland: "Earnings matter more than just about anything, except for maybe interest rates."*

**4. Supreme Court Fed Independence Ruling (Structural Positive)**
A 5-4 Supreme Court ruling protected Fed governors from being fired by the president without cause. The case stemmed from Trump's 2025 attempt to fire Governor Lisa Cook. The decision reinforces institutional stability and was cited by analysts as removing a tail risk for markets.
*Source: SCOTUSblog, AP News, TheStreet. Ruling: June 29; market reaction: June 30 session.*

**5. Consumer Confidence Miss, JOLTS Beat (Mixed Macro)**
Conference Board consumer confidence came in at 91.2 vs 94.2 consensus — below expectations, with "jobs hard to get" at 22.5% (highest since Jan 2021). However, JOLTS job openings rose to 7.59M vs 7.3M forecast, suggesting labor market resilience. Net effect: neutral for equities, modestly hawkish for rates.
*Source: CNBC. Releases: June 30, 10:00 AM ET.*
