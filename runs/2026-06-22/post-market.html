Why Markets Moved

1. Big Tech AI Talent Exodus Triggers Rotation (Dominant Driver)

Alphabet (GOOGL) fell 7% — its worst day in a year — erasing an estimated $225–270 billion in market cap after two high-profile AI researcher departures. Nobel laureate John Jumper (AlphaFold co-creator) left DeepMind for Anthropic, and Noam Shazeer (Gemini co-lead) departed for OpenAI within days of each other (CNBC, June 22). The exits amplified existing concerns about AI commoditization after Microsoft CEO Satya Nadella called the AI market "commoditized" in a weekend WSJ interview. Alphabet has raised $141 billion in debt and equity since October for AI infrastructure — investors are now questioning whether that spending builds a durable moat.

Contagion: Amazon -4.8%, Microsoft -3.0%, Meta -2.3%. Together these four mega-caps accounted for the bulk of the Nasdaq's 1.32% decline.

2. US-Iran Peace Breakthrough Crashes Oil

Brent crude fell 3.31% to $77.90 and WTI dropped 2.32% to $74.82 after weekend talks in Switzerland produced a roadmap for a final deal within 60 days, per mediators Qatar and Pakistan (Reuters, CNBC). The US Treasury issued a 60-day general license authorizing Iranian oil production, delivery, and sale through August 21 — including importation to the US with dollar payments (CNBC, Treasury announcement). VP JD Vance called the progress "great."

Supply estimates (ANZ, via Reuters): 2–3 million bpd restored in first 4 weeks, additional 2–3.5 million bpd potentially recoverable in Q3 if stability holds, but 1–2 million bpd permanently or semi-permanently lost. "Full restoration is unlikely this year" (ANZ). David Roche (Quantum Strategy) warned the apparent abundance "reflects inventory liquidation rather than a recovery in production."

3. Sector Rotation Accelerates: Out of Tech, Into Value, Industrials, Small Caps

The Russell 2000 closed at a record 3,004.40 — first time above 3,000. Caterpillar (+4%) single-handedly kept the Dow positive due to price-weighted index mechanics. The pattern is consistent with prior rotation episodes: AI infrastructure gets sold, cash recycles into non-tech sectors and small caps (Investing.com, InteractiveCrypto). Fed hawkishness from last week's meeting — 9 of 19 policymakers projecting a rate hike before year-end — accelerated the rotation out of duration-sensitive growth names.

4. Memory/Chip Sector Defies Tech Weakness

Micron (MU) rallied 4–7% to a record high ahead of Wednesday's earnings. Consensus: Q3 revenue ~$34.98–36.15 billion (~4× y/y), adjusted EPS $19.15–20.95 (MarketWatch, Zacks). SMCI surged 17% on Nvidia Vera Rubin NVL4 platform announcement + GF Securities upgrade to Buy ($48 PT). Intel +5%, AMD +2%. Philadelphia Semiconductor Index (SOX) +0.5%.
