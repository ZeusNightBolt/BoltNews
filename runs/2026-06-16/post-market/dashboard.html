Why Markets Moved

Wall Street closed higher on Tuesday, driven by two dominant forces: the U.S.–Iran peace agreement deflating the oil-risk premium, and FOMC meeting anticipation under new Chair Kevin Warsh.

The peace deal, brokered by Pakistan and confirmed by President Trump on Sunday, commits both sides to a ceasefire and reopens the Strait of Hormuz. The formal signing is expected Friday in Switzerland. Crude oil prices collapsed on the news — WTI fell from ~$84/bbl Friday to below $79/bbl Tuesday, a cumulative ~6% drop over two sessions. This removed the single largest near-term inflation impulse that had driven May CPI to 4.2% YoY headline. — TheStreet, Kiplinger, Kitco

The FOMC began its two-day meeting Tuesday, the first chaired by Kevin Warsh. Markets priced a near-certainty of a rate hold at 3.50%–3.75%, with the key question being whether the committee drops its easing bias for a neutral stance. The decision and updated Summary of Economic Projections drop Wednesday at 2:00 PM ET, followed by Warsh's first press conference at 2:30 PM. — FXStreet, Kiplinger

Technology led the rally despite TheStreet's intraday blog noting Nasdaq was negative at 2:58 PM — a late-session surge reversed the tape. Energy was the sole notable loser, down 3.6% as oil stocks repriced to the Iran deal. — Zacks, TheStreet
