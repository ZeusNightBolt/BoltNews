Contrarian Flags

1. "Extremely misplaced euphoria" has now partially corrected — but is it enough? The S&P 500 has pulled back ~2.6% from ATHs. Amrita Sen (Energy Aspect) called the market "sleepwalking into a recession" at ATHs. If the Iran war drags on through summer, the macro damage (oil, shipping, food prices) may not yet be fully priced. The selloff may be the START of repricing, not the end.

2. Rate hikes into a war economy: The market is pricing Fed rate HIKES while a major oil supply disruption rages. Historically, central banks ease during supply shocks. If the Fed actually hikes into this, it would be a policy error that amplifies recession risk. The contrarian bet: the Fed will NOT hike — markets are overpricing tightening, and bonds will rally when this is realized.

3. Credit market complacency: The Apollo and Morgan Stanley warnings about AI-driven debt supply are structural, not cyclical. The $800B hyperscaler capex in 2026 (and $1.16T in 2027) must be financed. If credit spreads widen on this supply alone — without a recession — it creates a negative feedback loop for equities. Yet spreads remain tight.

4. SpaceX IPO as a liquidity event: The largest IPO in history ($75B raise) debuts Friday. This will drain liquidity from the broader market as institutions rotate to participate. Combined with Alphabet's $85B equity raise and Meta's rumored offering, equity supply is surging at a moment when rate expectations are rising. This is a bearish setup for equities.

5. Bitcoin below 200-day MA: Historically a contrarian buy signal — the last two times BTC broke below its 200-DMA (2023, 2022), it marked significant bottoms. Downside may be limited from here, but correlated selloff with tech means crypto is not currently a hedge.
