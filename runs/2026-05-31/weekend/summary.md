## Equities: AI Infrastructure Supercycle Confirmed

**The Dell number that matters:** AI server revenue hit $16.1B in the quarter — for the first time exceeding PC revenue ($14.6B). That's a 757% YoY increase. Dell raised its full-year revenue outlook from $138-142B to $165-169B, with $60B specifically from AI servers. "The AI opportunity shows no signs of slowing." (Source: Reuters, CNBC — May 29, 2026)

This validates the entire AI capex thesis. HPE (+13.7%), SMCI (+8.6%) on sympathy. Broadcom (AVGO) reports Wednesday — expected to confirm >$100B in AI chip revenue for next year.

**But the rotation is narrowing.** NVDA continues to lag. Software names are mixed (Okta +8%, Autodesk -7%). Consumer discretionary led sectors over 5 days despite weakening consumer data.

**Technicals:** S&P 500 RSI above 70 — overbought. ~7% above 50-day MA. (Source: Schwab Market Update — May 29, 2026)

## Rates & Fed: Warsh's Quiet Revolution

**Kevin Warsh is preparing to fundamentally restructure how the Fed manages its $6.8 trillion balance sheet.** (Source: CNBC — May 22, 2026)

Key data:
- Fed balance sheet: $6.8T (23% of GDP) — 7x the pre-2008 share
- Fed research suggests up to **$2.1 trillion in cuts** are possible
- The overnight repo rate could **replace the federal funds rate** as primary policy tool
- Governor Barr opposed: "would undermine bank resilience, threaten financial stability"
- Dallas Fed's Logan: "any changes should be gradual and planned carefully"
- 10-year Treasury at 4.44% — Schwab: "Yields likely to remain elevated across the curve"
- KC Fed's Schmid: Inflation "most pressing risk"
- Powell to speak Sunday at JFK Profile in Courage Award (Source: Forth News — May 31, 2026)

**Implication:** The balance sheet debate creates policy uncertainty. Markets pricing rate hikes by March 2027; regime change could tighten through channels the market doesn't fully understand.

## Oil & Geopolitics: The Peace Premium

Brent crude's 19% May collapse to $92.05 is the biggest monthly drop since March 2020. Treasury Secretary Bessent said U.S. and Iran have "the makings of a deal." (Source: WSJ — May 29, 2026)

Trump: "meeting with my team to make a final determination." White House demands: Iran must dispose enriched uranium, renounce nuclear weapons, and fully reopen Strait of Hormuz.

**Asymmetry:** Oil has further to fall on a deal than to rise on failure — the 19% drop already reflects high probability.
